Twitter Newsfeed Analysis

We can evaluate Twitter-newsfeeds according to their ideological leaning!

Hi! My name is Philipp and I'm a Ph.D. student at the Graduate School of Decision Sciences in Germany. One of my supervisors, Pablo Barberá, built a tool to evaluate a user's Twitter-newsfeed based on the persons this user is following. Its result is a numeric value indicating the aggregated ideological leaning of the user's Twitter-friends. I want to briefly present some interesting insights this tool provided and explain how you might use this tool for yourself to analyze your own Twitter-newsfeed (if you use Twitter).

See e.g. below the estimate for various U.S. political elites and news outlets (e.g. Median House D. displays the median ideological leaning for newsfeeds of all U.S. house representatives belonging to the Democratic party).

As can be seen, the basic tendency of most polticial figures makes sense and the evaluation of news outlets is in accordance with discussions about partisan news (e.g. @FoxNews displaying a conservative newsfeed while the @nytimes is located on the liberal side). Interestingly, official members of the democratic party (in the form of house representatives or senate members) are associated with more extreme liberal newsfeeds than the news outlets associated with a liberal leaning (e.g. @msnbc). The opposite is true for Republican officials: political figures are often located on a more moderate scale than news outlets which are associated with a conservative tendency (@FoxNews).

Additionally, I present the aggregated results of users of this website who participated in a short survey (n=142, Last update: April 10th, 2019). I ask participants basic questions with regards to demographics and Twitter usage and classify persons into two groups for each variable for the sake of simplicity. In the picture below I then plotted the average of the newsfeed values of each of these groups in relation to their counterpart (e.g. persons who consider themselves liberal vs. persons who I classified as conservative).

×

As makes intuitively sense, persons who claim to be liberal are also associated with liberal newsfeeds and vice versa for conservatives (plot above, line 1). More interestingly, I find that the more active users in this sample have a more liberal newsfeed than the less active ones (active vs. occassional, line 2). An anticipated relationship can also be observed with regards to the age of participants: older people seem to have a more conservative newsfeed than younger ones confirming the saying...

"If you’re not a liberal when you’re 25, you have no heart. If you’re not a conservative by the time you’re 35, you have no brain."

Furthermore, participants who have at least completed highschool as well as female participants seem to have a more conservative news diet than participants without highschool or males respectively.

Overall my sample seems to be biased into a liberal direction compared to the newsfeed of the average Twitter user (green line in the above plot). I hope to balance out these sampling biases on the long run.

By the way, if you are using Twitter yourself, you can get your personal evaluation of your own Twitter-newsfeed here and contribute to my non-profit Ph.D. project with your particpation. The estimation works best for users following U.S. political elites. All you need to do is to authenticate my Twitter-app which evaluates your publicly available Twitter-friendlist.
